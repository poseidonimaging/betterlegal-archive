Certificate of Formation

The Certificate of Formation of a limited liability company (LLC) is a document filed with the Texas Secretary of State to formally establish your business as a separate legal entity. It identifies your company’s name, managers, and registered agent, and provides a mailing address for important correspondence from the State and others.

Company Agreement

While an LLC is legally established by filing a Certificate of Formation, it is imperative that you have a thorough Company Agreement that will govern your LLC’s business. Among other things, a Company Agreement identifies the members (owners) and managers of the LLC, including their respective ownership percentages and management rights, and establishes procedures for meetings, the admission or removal of members and managers, and the distribution of profits to members.

Banking Resolution and EIN

In order to establish a bank account, your bank may require you to provide a banking resolution and Employer Identification Number (EIN). A banking resolution identifies which managers and officers of your LLC can draft checks, deposit or withdraw funds, etc. Your LLC’s EIN, which is also required if you plan to have employees, is provided by the Internal Revenue Service.

Why set up an LLC?

LLCs are quickly becoming the most popular type of business entity in the State of Texas and across the country, and for good reason. Unlike sole proprietorships or partnerships, all of the owners and managers of an LLC are typically protected against personal liability for business debts and other liabilities (meaning creditors cannot pursue the personal assets of a manager or member to pay business debts or judgments). And unlike corporations, LLCs are very flexible from a tax and management perspective. An LLC is not subject to double taxation, meaning that all business income is “passed through” to the owners and reported on their personal income tax returns. In addition, LLCs are more easily-managed than corporations, which must adhere to certain corporate formalities and annual compliance requirements that can be a hassle to keep up with.
